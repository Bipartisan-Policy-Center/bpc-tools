The Federal Scholarship Tax Credit Program: How Many Taxpayers Need to Contribute for Large Scale Programs Serving Public School Students?

The Federal Scholarship Tax Credit (FSTC) program, enacted as part of the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, allows taxpayers to redirect up to $1,700 of their federal tax liability as donations to scholarship-granting organizations serving K–12 public school students. This calculator estimates how many taxpayer donors would be needed to fund scholarships at scale in any state — and what share of eligible taxpayers that represents. Adjust the scholarship cost, target student population, and participation method to explore different program scenarios.

Based on NCES Fall 2023 enrollment, NCES FRPL rates, Census ACS income data, and IRS SOI TY2022 returns.

300% AMI (proxy)

The FSTC program uses 300% of HUD Area Median Income, which varies by metropolitan area and county — there is no single statewide number. No pre-built state-level dataset exists for this threshold. This calculator uses the Census ACS share of K–12 public school children in households below 300% of the federal poverty line as a conservative proxy (ACS Table B17024 / NCES ACS-ED). Because the poverty line is lower than AMI in most areas, this proxy still understates the true AMI-eligible population.
FRPL / Economically Disadvantaged

FRPL covers households at or below 185% of the federal poverty level (NCES CCD 2022-23). Because the FSTC program's eligibility threshold is 300% of HUD Area Median Income — which is higher than the poverty line in most areas — FRPL is a lower-bound proxy that understates the true eligible population.

Method B: 300% AMI proxyThe FSTC program uses 300% of HUD Area Median Income, which varies by county and family size — no single statewide figure exists. Method C: Percent of eligible taxpayersSets the student population from the donor side: a given percentage of eligible taxpayers each contribute $1,700, generating a total fund (contributors × $1,700), from which the number of students served is derived (⌊ total funds ÷ scholarship cost ⌋).

Federal FSTC · §25F · Guidance Note

$1,700 Credit Limit: Per Return, Not Per Person

An important interpretation note on the maximum donor contribution.

⚠️
Anticipated guidance on married filing jointlyCalculator assumes a limit of $1,700 per return, which means that married-filing-jointly couples would receive $1,700, not $3,400. Final Treasury rules could impact this.
Implication for this calculator: All donor and program-size estimates in this tool assume $1,700 per contributor, consistent with the anticipated per-return interpretation. If guidance ultimately permits $3,400 for joint filers, the number of donors required would be lower than shown here — meaning these estimates are conservative under that alternative interpretation.
